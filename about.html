Our Mission

Freestyle Movement is a registered non-profit company that supports Edmonton Bboys and Bgirls through providing regular practice sessions, teaching classes and workshops, as well as hosting competitions. We hope to see the Bboy community grow in size and in skill as people from different backgrounds come together and experience the culture of Hip Hop.

Our Story

Every dream has a first step, and for us this is it. From ideas ranging from dance studio cafes, to having large open warehouse space for competitions, we at Freestyle Movement wanted to make an impact. United behind an idea, we took the first step to begin formalizing our Friday night practice sessions, teaching Hip Hop and Breaking workshops, and beginning to host dance competitions.

The Team

Hubert

President

Starting out as a bboy in 2007, Hubert has competed in numerous breaking competitions and performed along side Crazy Time Crew. Bboying is what transformed him from being a shy and slightly awkward boy into who he is now. The idea for a dance-studio-bubble-tea-cafe hybrid business came from a visit to local bubble tea shop, and from there Freestyle Movement was eventually born.

Pranaya

Vice President – External

Pranaya began his journey by learning through internet videos then slowly integrating into the community. He was inspired by breaking performances and events so he began to take dance seriously. The involvement with the dance community eventually led him to becoming a founding member of Freestyle Movement.

Patricia

Vice President - Internal

Patricia was initially an extra hand when it came to the company start-up including volunteer recruitment and the website creation. From there, she has been assisting in process development and event planning to fulfill Freestyle Movement Dance Company’s vision and goals.

Elaine

Finance Executive

Beats and beans are her thing, Elaine or better known as Empress, began her journey in dance in 2013. She has explored a variety of forms, Tango, Belly, Hip Hop and wanting to try something more physically demanding she started BGirling it in May 2016. Her journey has led her to become more involved with the dance community and thus becoming a founding member of Freestyle Movement.
